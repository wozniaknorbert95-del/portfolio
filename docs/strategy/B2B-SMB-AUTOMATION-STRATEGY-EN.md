# B2B SMB Automation Strategy

## Executive Summary

FlexGrafik operates three concurrent tracks: (1) a Rotterdam-based branding/print shop serving ZZP/SMB clients, (2) a production-grade AI ecosystem built to automate that shop, and (3) a world-class AI Systems Architect portfolio aimed at enterprise engineering buyers. Track 2 was built as internal tooling. It is now robust enough to be productized and sold as Track 4: a B2B SMB automation service. This strategy defines how Track 4 is positioned, priced, and delivered without diluting Tracks 1 or 3.

**The core thesis:** SMB owners do not buy AI. They buy time, leads, and professional appearance. The technology underneath (Agent OS, jadzia-core, SSoT pipelines) is invisible to the buyer.

**The critical correction:** The previous strategy attempted to build a full-service agency (websites, e-commerce, marketing) with a 4-package matrix. That spreads too thin, requires too much client interaction, and kills the enterprise track (Track 3) with €97/mo maintenance overhead.

**The revised thesis:** Launch with **one product** — AI Lead Qualification ("Inbox Killer") — deployed in **48 hours** using Make/Zapier + jadzia-core LLM classification. One product, one price, one promise. If it validates in 72 hours, scale. If not, pivot immediately.

**Freeze for 14 days:** Website builds, e-commerce, and complex systems. Only Inbox Killer ships.

The seller (Norbert Wozniak) maintains his AI Systems Architect credibility by treating this as a *48h deployment proof*, not a pivot to web dev.

## 5. Product Strategy

### 5.1 Productization Principle

**One product, one price, one promise.** No packages. No matrix. The product is a **48-hour deployment** that solves one specific, painful, expensive problem.

**Freeze for 14 days:** Website builds, e-commerce, complex Next.js sites, and anything requiring client content/assets. These require client cooperation (API keys, copy, logos, approvals) that breaks the 48h promise. Only Inbox Killer ships because the client only needs to connect their inbox — everything else is automated.

### 5.2 The Product: AI Lead Qualification ("Inbox Killer")

**The problem:** SMB owners receive 50–200 emails/day. 80% is noise (newsletters, spam, cold outreach, support). The 20% that matters — hot leads, urgent client requests, partnership opportunities — gets buried. The owner either hires a VA (€800–1,500/month) or misses opportunities.

**The solution:** An AI pipeline that reads the inbox, classifies every email in real time, surfaces hot leads with summary + suggested reply, and archives noise. Deployed in 48 hours.

| Spec | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Price** | €497 setup + €147/month |
| **Delivery time** | 48 hours |
| **Input required** | Client connects email (Gmail/Outlook) via OAuth. One 15-min onboarding call. |
| **What client gets** | Dashboard with: inbox zero score, hot leads queue, auto-generated reply drafts, daily summary email |
| **What you build** | Make/Zapier workflow + jadzia-core LLM classification + simple Next.js dashboard |
| **Monthly recurring** | Continuous classification tuning, new intent categories, reply template improvements |
| **Cancel anytime** | Client owns their data. No lock-in. |

**Why €147/mo and not €97/mo:** This is a VA replacement, not a website maintenance fee. A VA costs €800–1,500/mo. At €147/mo, the ROI is clear in the first week. The higher price also means fewer clients needed to fund Track 2 and Track 3.

### 5.3 Launch Incentive (first 5 clients)

- First month free (pay setup €497, month 1 is €0)
- Personal onboarding call (15 min, not 1 hour)
- Priority support via WhatsApp for 30 days
- **Goal:** validate that 48h delivery is real, collect testimonials, stress-test the system

### 5.4 What the Client Sees vs. What You Build

| Client Sees | You Build With (existing modules) |
|---|---|
| "My inbox is clean every morning" | Make/Zapier webhook + jadzia-core LLM classification |
| "I never miss a hot lead" | Intent scoring + priority queue + notification rules |
| "AI drafts replies for me" | jadzia-core multi-LLM gateway with custom templates |
| "It improves every week" | Flex-VCMS monthly scan + model fine-tuning feedback loop |
| "I see everything in one dashboard" | Next.js + Tailwind + existing Skeleton components |
| "It was live in 2 days" | Pre-built workflow templates + SSoT client config (repos.yaml style) |

### 5.5 Why Inbox Killer First (and not Website Modernization)

| Dimension | Website Modernization | Inbox Killer |
|---|---|---|
| Client input required | High (content, assets, approvals, feedback loops) | Low (OAuth + 15 min call) |
| Delivery risk | High (design subjectivity, scope creep) | Low (workflow is deterministic) |
| Time to value | 2–3 weeks | 48 hours |
| Recurring justification | "Hosting + updates" (weak) | "AI that learns your business" (strong) |
| Track 3 impact | €97/mo x 10 = €970/mo, but hours consumed | €147/mo x 5 = €735/mo, mostly automated |
| Enterprise credibility | Neutral | High (demonstrates LLM + automation at scale) |
| KFA fit | Medium (accountants don't care about websites) | **High** (accountants LIVE in their inbox) |

## 6. Go-to-Market Strategy

### 6.1 Channel 1: LinkedIn (organic)

**Not a sales channel. A credibility channel.**

- 2 posts/week, English
- Content pillars: (a) Behind the Build — how systems work in business terms, (b) The Automation Principle — one concept with real numbers, (c) KFA Journey — documenting the sales/delivery process in real time
- Every post ends with a soft CTA: "Inbox full of noise? I clear 80% of it in 48 hours and surface the leads that actually matter. DM me for a demo."
- No website talk. No "modernize your site." Only inbox, leads, and time saved.
- No hashtags like #vibeCoding. Use: #SMBautomation #digitaltransformation #smallbusiness #AIforbusiness

### 6.2 Channel 2: KFA Referral Network

**Primary distribution channel for Track 4 — but NOT the only channel.**

**Critical risk:** If KFA says no on June 6, the entire Month 1 plan collapses. This is a single-point-of-failure. Mitigation: run the 72h validation test (Channel 3) BEFORE the KFA meeting. Arrive at June 6 with evidence: "3 businesses already asked for demos this week."

**KFA offer:**
- **Product:** Inbox Killer only. Not websites, not e-commerce.
- **His incentive:** He looks like the hero who discovered a time-saving tool for his 600 clients.
- **Your incentive:** Zero acquisition cost for first 10+ clients.
- **Partner deal:** KFA clients get first month free (setup €497, month 1 = €0). KFA gets nothing upfront — he wins by being the trusted advisor who found the tool.
- **Next meeting (June 6):** Present the product live. Show his own inbox if he consents. Close or learn objections.
- **If KFA says yes:** Deliver to him first (he becomes case study #1). Then ask: "Which of your clients complain most about email overload?"
- **If KFA says no:** Ask specifically what is missing. Is it price? Trust? Product fit? Use the answer to pivot. Do NOT wait 30 days — the 72h test already proved organic demand exists independently.

### 6.3 Channel 3: 72-Hour Validation Test (CRITICAL)

**Do NOT build landing pages, scanners, or complex funnels yet.** Before writing a single line of code for the public, validate that SMB owners actually want this.

**The test:**
1. Write ONE LinkedIn post: *"Your inbox is 80% noise. I built an AI system that clears the clutter in 48 hours and surfaces only the leads that matter. First 5 demos are free. DM me 'INBOX' and I'll show you your own data."*
2. Send the same message to 5 friendly business owners (not KFA — separate test).
3. Wait 72 hours.
4. **If ≥3 people ask for a demo:** The message resonates. Proceed to build the landing page.
5. **If <3 people ask for a demo:** The message is too technical or the channel doesn't work. Pivot: try different copy (lead-focused, not AI-focused) or switch to KFA-only distribution. The product may be right, but the messaging or channel needs work.

**Why this matters:** The previous strategy assumed organic LinkedIn would work. It might not. The 72h test costs zero euros and saves 2 weeks of building a funnel nobody uses.

**Channel 3b (after validation): Free Inbox Diagnostic**
- Once validated, build a simple tool: "How much is your inbox costing you?"
- Input: connect Gmail/Outlook → output: noise percentage, hot lead count, time-wasted estimate
- Delivered via email to capture lead information
- Psychology: quantified loss. "You're losing 12 hours/week to email noise. Here's the proof."

### 6.4 Channel 4: Local Network (Rotterdam)

- Existing FlexGrafik clients (branding, print, wrapping) are ZZP/BV owners
- Every invoice, every delivery, every conversation is a soft pitch opportunity
- "By the way — we also modernize websites now. Same quality you expect from our print work."

## 7. Brand Architecture Decision

### 7.1 Why Separate `services.flexgrafik.nl`?

| Option | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subpage (`flexgrafik.nl/services`) | Strong SEO for main domain | Dilutes brand; flexgrafik.nl is print/design, not tech | ❌ |
| Subdomain (`services.flexgrafik.nl`) | Separate tracking, separate build, unified brand | Extra DNS, slightly more maintenance | ✅ |
| New domain (`automate.nl`) | Clean separation, easy A/B testing | No brand recognition, extra cost | ❌ |
| Use portfolio (`portfolio.flexgrafik.nl/business`) | One codebase, one deploy | Dilutes AI Systems Architect positioning; SMB visitors see LangGraph content and bounce | ❌ |

**Decision:** Subdomain `services.flexgrafik.nl`. Keeps FlexGrafik brand equity. Allows separate analytics, separate conversion tracking. Can be built with the same Next.js stack but independent deploy.

### 7.2 Why Keep `portfolio.flexgrafik.nl` Untouched?

The AI Systems Architect portfolio is a long-term asset. It attracts:
- Enterprise consulting leads (day rate €800–1,500)
- Conference speaking invitations
- GitHub visibility and recruitment interest
- Technical credibility that validates the B2B service

If you mix SMB landing pages into the portfolio, you:
- Confuse enterprise buyers ("Is he a web dev or an AI architect?")
- Weaken SEO for both audiences
- Create maintenance complexity

**Rule:** `portfolio.flexgrafik.nl` never mentions ZZP packages. `services.flexgrafik.nl` never mentions LangGraph. The bridge is the Norbert Wozniak story, not the UI.

## 12. Strategic Principles (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Track 3 is sacred.** The AI Systems Architect portfolio never carries SMB pricing, packages, or ZZP language. It is a 6-month positioning asset.

2. **One product, one price, one promise.** Inbox Killer only. No packages. No matrix. No custom quotes.

3. **Show, don't tell.** Every claim is backed by a working system, a metric, or a client result. No AI buzzwords in SMB-facing copy.

4. **Client approves everything.** Zasada 11 applies to Track 4. HITL gates for all deploys. Client sees and approves before anything goes live.

5. **Validate before building.** The 72-hour test is not optional. It is the first line of defense against building something nobody wants.

6. **48 hours is a promise, not a stretch goal.** If a client needs custom work that breaks the 48h timeline, it is a separate engagement. The product ships in 48h or it is not the product.

7. **KFA is primary, but not only.** The 72h test proves organic demand exists without KFA. If KFA says no, Track 4 does not die.

8. **Track 3 time is non-negotiable.** Track 4 cannot consume hours that belong to Track 3 (enterprise positioning, portfolio growth, GitHub activity). Track 4 must be mostly automated after delivery.

9. **Document everything.** Every session, every build, every client interaction becomes a handoff doc, a log entry, a case study, a LinkedIn post. Knowledge compounds.
